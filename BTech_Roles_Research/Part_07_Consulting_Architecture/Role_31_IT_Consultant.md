## Overview

**What is an IT Consultant?**

An IT Consultant is a professional who provides expert technology advice to client organizations to help them solve business problems, improve processes, implement technology solutions, and optimize IT operations. Unlike product company employees who build products, consultants work on diverse projects across multiple clients and industries.

**Key Characteristics:**
- **Client-facing:** Work directly with client stakeholders
- **Project-based:** Move between different projects and clients
- **Varied work:** Exposure to multiple industries, technologies, problems
- **Deliverable-focused:** Presentations, reports, implementation plans
- **Travel-intensive:** Frequent travel to client sites
- **Business + Technology:** Need both technical and business skills

**GOOD NEWS for Freshers:** Unlike architect roles, you CAN start as an IT Consultant right after B.Tech! Many consulting firms hire fresh graduates as Analysts or Associate Consultants.

- Discuss risks and mitigation plans
- Get approval for next phase

**10:30 AM - 12:30 PM:** Workshop with client IT team
- Design session for new microservices architecture
- Whiteboarding technical approach
- Document decisions and action items

**12:30 PM - 1:30 PM:** Working lunch with team
- Review team's deliverables
- Provide feedback on analysis
- Plan week's activities

**1:30 PM - 4:00 PM:** Deep work
- Create architecture design document
- Review team's PowerPoint slides
- Update project plan in MS Project

**4:00 PM - 5:00 PM:** Call with firm leadership
- Practice update to partner
- Discuss resource needs
- Budget review

**5:00 PM - 7:00 PM:** Client dinner
- Relationship building
- Discuss future opportunities
- Understand client's strategic priorities

**Tuesday: Client Site**

**9:00 AM - 12:00 PM:** Technical assessment
- Interview client's development team
- Review existing architecture
- Identify gaps and opportunities

**12:00 PM - 2:00 PM:** Team collaboration
- Work with analysts on data gathering
- Review their Excel models
- Guide approach for analysis

**2:00 PM - 5:00 PM:** Create presentation
- Summarize findings from week
- Create executive summary
- Design PowerPoint deck for CTO presentation

**5:00 PM - 6:00 PM:** Internal team meeting
- Sync with other consultants on project
- Share learnings
- Resolve cross-work stream issues

**Wednesday: Firm Office (Bangalore)**

**9:00 AM - 10:00 AM:** Proposal development
- Sales call for new opportunity
- Brainstorm approach with partner
- Estimate effort and cost

**10:00 AM - 12:00 PM:** Internal training
- Attend AWS certification training
- Learn new service offerings
- Network with colleagues

**12:00 PM - 2:00 PM:** Proposal writing
- Write technical approach section
- Create staffing plan

**2:00 PM - 5:00 PM:** Project work (remote)
- Finalize deliverables
- Quality review
- Prepare for Friday presentation

**5:00 PM - 6:00 PM:** Mentoring
- One-on-one with junior analyst
- Career guidance

**Thursday: Client Site**

**9:00 AM - 12:00 PM:** Vendor evaluation
- Meet with cloud vendors (AWS, Azure)

**12:00 PM - 2:00 PM:** Cost-benefit analysis
- Build financial model in Excel
- Calculate TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)

- Write recommendations report

- New industry every project

**Travel Stress:**
- Away from home frequently

- Hotel living
- Health impact (irregular meals, lack of exercise)

### Stress Mitigation

**High Compensation:**
- Good salaries for stress level

**Rapid Learning:**
- Massive skill development

## Consulting Culture & Lifestyle

### Work Hours

**Typical Week:**
- Monday: Travel to client site (morning)
- Monday-Thursday: Client site (9 AM - 7 PM, sometimes later)
- Thursday: Travel back home (evening)
- Friday: Firm office or remote (9 AM - 6 PM)

**Total Hours:** 50-60 hours/week normally, 70-80 during peaks

### Travel Expectations

**By Level:**
- Analyst (0-2 years): 60-80% travel
- Consultant (2-4 years): 50-70% travel
- Manager (4-7 years): 40-60% travel
- Senior Manager+ (7+ years): 30-50% travel (more choice)

**Post-COVID:** More remote work, but travel still significant

### Dress Code

**Client Site:**
- Business formal (suit, tie for men; formal for women)
- Varies by client culture
- Always professional

**Firm Office:**
- Business casual (Friday casual in some firms)
- Smart casual (post-COVID flexibility)

### Team Culture

**Collaborative:**
- Work in teams (3-10 people per project)
- Support each other
- Knowledge sharing

**Competitive:**
- Stack ranking in performance reviews
- Compete for promotions
- High performers rewarded

**Social:**
- Team dinners and outings
- Networking events
- Firm-sponsored events

### Up-or-Out Culture

**Performance Review:**
- Annual reviews (some firms bi-annual)
- Rated on curve (forced ranking)
- Promoted or asked to leave (up-or-out)

**Promotion Timeline:**
- Analyst ‚Üí Consultant: 2-3 years
- Consultant ‚Üí Manager: 2-3 years
- Manager ‚Üí Senior Manager: 2-3 years
- Senior Manager ‚Üí Partner: 3-5 years

**Attrition:**
- High (30-40% annual attrition in some firms)
- Many leave voluntarily for industry
- Some pushed out due to performance

- Consulting values tech background
- Highlight cross-functional projects
- Demonstrate business thinking
- Prepare for case interviews
- Target technology consulting roles

- With 2-3 years experience: Consultant level
- With 4-6 years experience: Senior Consultant/Manager level

- Build relationships
- Ask lots of questions

- Deliver excellent work
- Seek feedback constantly
- Network within firm
- Develop specialization
- Manage work-life balance
